Engine Overhauls

Does your rig billow smoke? Does your rig's engine make strange sounds when starting, stopping, while idle or accelerating? Have people ever suggested that you get your engine overhauled? Well, maybe you should have it looked at by a professional mechanic, but what actually is an engine overhaul, anyway? That is a good question to ask, and here is the answer.

There are a few ways or terms used to describe an engine overhaul, for example, some auto service centres term it an 'in-chassis' rebuild. This happens when mechanics don't have to take the whole engine out to rebuild and repair it, or they don't overhaul the whole engine, just components of it such as replacing the pistons rings, cylinder liners or all pistons, if they need replacing. An in-chassis rebuild will surely get your engine back in shape, and its performance will be as good as or better than when it was new.

Electrical Issues

What issues can we fix?

The electronic and electrical systems in a truck are complex and intricate and perform the role of sending information and power to every system present in the truck. Heating and cooling systems, the engine, transmission, power accessories, brakes, along with any other system relies on the electronic and electrical systems to operate at optimum levels.

Broken or loose wiring, incorrect pin fits, broken or corroded switches or connectors can result in sporadic power or a system that becomes completely inoperative - meaning a dead truck.
